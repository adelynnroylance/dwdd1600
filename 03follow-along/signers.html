Who Were the Signers

Famous Signers

Several signers of the Declaration of Independence became well-known figures in American history. Here are some of the most famous

John Hancock
He was the president of the Continental Congress and famously signed the Declaration with a large, bold signature. His name became synonymous with “signature” in American culture.
Thomas Jefferson
The principal author of the Declaration of Independence, Jefferson went on to become the 3rd president of the United States.
Benjamin Franklin
A well-known statesman, scientist, and diplomat, Franklin was one of the most prominent Founding Fathers. He played a key role in securing French support for the Revolution.
John Adams
A leader in the American Revolution and the 2nd president of the United States, Adams was a vocal advocate for independence and played a major role in the Continental Congress.
Samuel Adams
A cousin of John Adams, Samuel was a strong advocate for colonial rights and played a crucial role in organizing resistance to British rule, including the Boston Tea Party.
Richard Henry Lee
He introduced the resolution for independence to the Continental Congress, which eventually led to the Declaration of Independence.
Francis Hopkinson
A signer from New Jersey, he was also a lawyer, artist, and musician. He designed some of the early American currency and the flag of New Jersey.
Thomas Paine
While not a signer, his pamphlet *Common Sense* was incredibly influential in rallying support for independence.

There were 56 signers in total, and while not all of them became household names, many of them played pivotal roles in shaping the new nation. Some of them went on to serve in important political and military positions, while others quietly returned to their previous lives after the Revolution.
