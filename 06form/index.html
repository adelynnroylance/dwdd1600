Piercings

Earlobe Piercing
The most common type of piercing, typically done with a small stud or hoop. Heals relatively quickly.
Cartilage Piercing
Located on the upper part of the ear. Takes longer to heal and may be more prone to irritation.
Nose Piercing
Usually done on one nostril, though septum piercings are also popular. Jewelry options include studs, hoops, and rings.
Eyebrow Piercing
A horizontal piercing through the eyebrow, often decorated with a curved barbell.
Tongue Piercing
A piercing through the center of the tongue. Heals quickly but requires special care to avoid infection.
Belly Button Piercing
Popular for aesthetic appeal, especially during warmer months. Can take several months to heal fully.
Lip Piercing
Includes labret, Monroe, and snake bite styles. Placement and jewelry vary based on the look desired.
Industrial Piercing
A double piercing connected with a single barbell, typically across the upper ear cartilage.
Dermal Piercing
A single-point surface piercing that sits flat against the skin, often appearing like a small gem or stud.
